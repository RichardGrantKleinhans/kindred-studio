## Pedagogical Philosophy Resources (Free or Partial Free Access)

### Montessori (AMI)

- **AMI Digital — Free educational resources (ages 3-6):** [Link](https://montessoridigital.org)
- **AMI Standards:** [Link](https://www.amiusa.org/ami-standards)
- **AMI/USA Membership ($125/year):** [Link](https://www.amiusa.org/membership) — provides full digital access to educational resources, academic papers, journals, and discussion forums
- **Maria Montessori’s original writings:** Public domain (pre-1929), available on Project Gutenberg and Internet Archive
- **Note:** The philosophy and core principles are extensively documented in open sources. AMI membership provides curriculum-to-standards alignment documentation useful for Kindred Studio integration.

### Reggio Emilia

- **Free webinars:** [Link](https://www.reggiochildren.it/en/rc/education/) — watch as often as you like for one month after access
- **Foundation newsletter signup:** [Link](https://www.frchildren.org/en)
- **Core principles and free PDF checklists:** [Link](https://reggio-inspired.com/reggio-emilia-approach)
- **Reggio Emilia Approach overview:** [Link](https://www.reggiochildren.it/en/reggio-emilia-approach)
- **NAREA (North American Reggio Emilia Alliance):** [Link](https://www.reggioalliance.org/reggio-children/)
- **Note:** The philosophy itself is openly documented. Specific implementation documentation from Reggio Children is proprietary. The approach is not a scripted curriculum — it is a framework for emergent, child-led project work.

### Waldorf / Steiner

- **Waldorf Resources (open access teaching guides):** [Link](https://www.waldorf-resources.org)
- **National Steiner education association publications:** Available through country-specific Waldorf/Steiner education associations
- **Note:** Core curriculum outlines published freely. Emphasis on arts integration, developmental stages, and main lesson blocks.

### Forest School / Friluftsliv

- **UK Forest School Association — Principles:** [Link](https://www.forestschoolassociation.org)
- **Six principles of Forest School:** Published openly by FSA
- **Note:** Implementation guides may require FSA membership. The principles and practitioner guidelines are freely available.

### International Baccalaureate (IB)

- **Programme documentation:** Free educator account at [ibo.org](https://www.ibo.org)
- **Note:** Framework descriptions and programme standards are accessible with free registration. Full implementation guides may require school membership.

### Classical Education (Trivium)

- **Well-Trained Mind (resource hub):** [Link](https://welltrainedmind.com)
- **Note:** The Trivium (grammar, logic, rhetoric) is a historical framework with extensive open documentation. Primary sources and Great Books lists are publicly available.

### Competency-Based Education

- **Aurora Institute (formerly iNACOL):** [Link](https://aurora-institute.org)
- **Note:** Frameworks, research, and policy documentation on competency-based education are widely published in open access.

## Indigenous Language and Cultural Resources

### Cherokee (ᏣᎳᎩ)

- **Cherokee Nation Language Department:** [Link](https://language.cherokee.org)
- **Cherokee fonts (free, Unicode):** [Link](https://language.cherokee.org/fonts-and-keyboards/cherokee-fonts/)
- **Cherokee Immersion School:** [Link](https://language.cherokee.org/cherokee-immersion-school/)
- **Eastern Band — Kituwah Academy:** [Link](https://www.ebci.gov/department-of-education/)
- **Unicode blocks:** U+13A0–U+13FF (Cherokee), U+AB70–U+ABBF (Cherokee Supplement)
- **Note:** iOS and Android have native Cherokee keyboard support. Claude’s Cherokee generation capability is limited — Kindred Studio generates lesson frameworks in English for Cherokee-speaking teachers to translate and adapt.

### Övdalska (Elfdalian)

- **Älvdalska bytes mot armhävningar / Course in Elfdalian (Facebook group):** Active community with admin Björn Rehnström
- **Övdals-Byönn Language Prize:** Annual award administered by Älvdalens kommun, funded by Coop Mitt
- **Note:** Endangered North Germanic language with approximately 2,500 speakers in Älvdalen, Sweden. Nasal vowels (ogonek: Ąą, Ęę, Įį, Ųų), eth (Ðð), and unique phonology distinguish it from Swedish.

### Māori (Te Reo Māori)

- **Tāhūrangi (NZ curriculum hub):** [Link](https://tahurangi.education.govt.nz)
- **Note:** Te Whāriki curriculum is bicultural by design, integrating Te Reo Māori throughout.

## How These Sources Are Used in Kindred Studio

When a teacher selects a pedagogical philosophy in Kindling, the AI generation engine adapts its system prompt to align with that philosophy’s principles, assessment approach, and values. The curriculum sources listed here inform the design of those system prompts and ensure alignment with established educational standards.

No curriculum content is reproduced directly. Sources are used as reference material for pedagogical alignment only.
